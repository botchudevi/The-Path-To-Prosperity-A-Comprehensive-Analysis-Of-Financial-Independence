    About

    The journey to financial independence can seem daunting and complex. Many people wonder, “How do I become financially independent?” and “What is true financial independence?” This article aims to provide a comprehensive guide to achieving your financial independence goals.

    FINANACIAL INDEPENDENCE

    Financial independence is a state where an individual or household has accumulated sufficient financial resources to cover its living expenses without having to depend on active employment or work to earn money in order to maintain its current lifestyle.

    • Having a dependable income and feeling in control of your finances.
    • Being on track to meeting your financial goal .
    • Having enough money saved up in case something goes wrong.

    The true meaning of financial freedom is the freedom to continue to live a decent lifestyle for a sustainable period of time. This is a state where you have enough funds to sustain yourself without hampering your health and well-being.

    FI vs FIRE

    Financial independence (FI) and FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) are closely related concepts but with some distinctions:

    Financial Independence (FI)

    This refers to achieving a state where your passive income covers your expenses, giving you the freedom to choose how you spend your time without being reliant on traditional employment. Financial independence focuses on building wealth and achieving financial security, often through saving, investing, and living within one's means.

    - FI is about cash flow
    - Moderate savings(15% to 45%)
    - lifestyle-moderate to comfortable
    - Cash flow investing
    - Retire when cash flow allows
    - Live from cash flow
    - Growning net worth
    - True financial independence

    FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early)

    FIRE takes the concept of financial independence a step further by aiming to achieve financial independence at a relatively young age, typically in one's 30s or 40s, allowing for early retirement or pursuing alternative career paths. FIRE proponents often adopt aggressive saving and investment strategies to accelerate the journey to financial independence.

    - FIRE is about accumulation
    - Aggressive savings(50%)
    - Lifestyle-extremely frugal throughout the entire time
    - Accumulate a stash of money
    - Retire early
    - Withdraw from stash
    - Declining network
    - Hope to not run out of money
